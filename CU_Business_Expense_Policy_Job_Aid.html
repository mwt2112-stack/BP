Business Expense Policy
Quick Reference Guide

⚠ Revised March 30, 2026 IRS Accountable Plan · Sponsored Project Compliance
The University will reimburse employees and students for expenditures made while conducting University business. The majority of expenses should be purchased through University approved purchasing paths — not directly by the employee. Where reimbursement applies, all expenses require a valid, documented business purpose. The more restrictive policy always governs — whether University, department, grant agency, or donor.

Accountability
Roles & Responsibilities
👤
Payee
Incurs the expense. Responsible for receipts, business purpose documentation, timely submission (10 days target; 120 day max), and completing or verifying the Expense Report in Concur.
📋
Preparer Delegate
Prepares Expense Reports on behalf of others. Confirms nature of each expenditure, documentation completeness, account accuracy, and proper segregation.
✅
Approver (Financial / SBO)
Must be senior to the Payee. Certifies expenses are appropriate, valid, policy-compliant, and within budget. Cannot approve own expenses. SBO approval required for exceptions.
💳
Accounts Payable
Processes all approved Expense Reports, issues payments, performs audits, assists with policy questions, and reviews exception/adjustment requests.
Key Rule
Under no circumstances may an individual approve their own report. Deans and V.P.s must have reports signed by the appropriate approver. If the Financial Approver reports to the Payee, an ADHOC approver (DAF or SBO) must be inserted.
IRS Requirements
Receipts & Documentation
What a Receipt Must Include
  • Date of purchase
  • Vendor name
  • Itemized list with unit prices
  • Total amount
  • Proof of payment (card type, last 4 digits, "Paid," or zero balance)
When Receipts Are Required
  • Always: air, lodging, rental car
  • All expenses ≥ $50
  • < $50: Missing Receipt Affidavit + Financial Approval
  • Supplies/equipment < $500: reimbursable
  • $500–$2,500: requires Financial Approval
  • > $2,500: requires Purchasing approval
Business Purpose Standard
Must be specific enough for a third-party reviewer. "Dinner with donor to discuss alumni event ideas" ✓ — "Dinner with donor" ✗. Expense date in Concur should match the transaction date or debit/credit card payment date.

Allowable Charges
Entertainment Expenses

The University will pay for approved, necessary and reasonable entertainment expenses directly related to University business, conducted for the benefit of external individuals. Entertainment includes theatre, sporting events, and recreational outings. These may not be charged to a sponsored research grant.

✓ Reimbursable Events
  • Dissertation defense
  • Promotion / Tenure / Sabbatical
  • Welcoming or leaving the University
  • Years of Service / Retirement
  • Graduation of an employee/student
  • Publication or performance opening
  • Recognition of a business achievement
  • Departmental holiday celebration
  • Collective birthday celebrations
  • Congratulations on business-related achievement
✕ Not Reimbursable
  • Baby / adoption showers
  • Engagements, weddings, anniversaries
  • Individual birthday celebrations
  • Congratulations on personal (non-business) achievements
SBO Approval Required When
Entertainment expenses exceed $1,500 total for the event, or $75 per person. Payee justification required alongside SBO sign-off.
Documentation Required
Receipt / InvoiceDate, location, items purchased, total amount, proof of payment.
Business PurposeSpecific focused purpose — e.g. "recruitment event for assistant professor of psychiatry" or "baseball game with potential donor." Vague purposes like "to foster collaboration" are insufficient.
Attendee ListList all attendees (or headcount if >10) with each person's relationship to the University.

Gift Policy
Gifts to Non-Employees
Gifts to non-employees must have a valid business purpose (e.g., business development, recognition of support for the University). Aggregate value must not exceed $600 per calendar year. Gifts to vendors are not reimbursable. All taxable/reportable non-employee payments must be processed via CU Marketplace, not Expense Reports.
Documentation Required
StandardDate, items purchased, total, proof of payment, approved business purpose.
1099 Reporting Threshold (2026)If gifts to a non-employee total $2,000 or more in calendar year 2026: recipient's name, home address, and Social Security number required for Form 1099. If $2,000 or less, note that gifts do not exceed $2,000 for the year.
SBO RequiredIf gift value exceeds $600. Contact Accounts Payable to discuss taxation when approaching or exceeding this level.

Commuting Exception
If a department approves commuting costs, the employee must be informed the reimbursement is taxable income and will be reported to Payroll for Form W-2. Exceptions for employees assigned outside commuting distance require VP for Human Resources or University Controller approval before travel.
Cash Advances
Advances & Reconciliation

Advances may be requested to cover conferences or research participant payments. Requests must include a description of circumstances and a brief budget. Non-employee student advances must be completed and reconciled by an employee.

MilestoneTimeframeConsequence of Inaction

Hard reconciliation deadline Within 90 days of event end date Must reconcile before a new advance can be issued
Reminder emails begin 60 days past last expense date (weekly) Weekly emails from Accounts Payable until reconciled
Final unreconciled deadline 120 days past last expense date Advance privileges suspended; reported as W-2 income; taxes withheld
Important
Once an unsettled advance is added to Form W-2, it cannot be reversed. If expenses are less than the advance, reimburse the University via personal check to natural account 65399.
Other Policy Areas
Additional Rules
Key Rules by Category
Subscriptions, Memberships & Dues Allowed if appropriate to position and duties and benefit the University. Use employee's CU address when possible. Optional contributions/donations within a membership are not reimbursable. The University does not reimburse airline clubs, country clubs, civic clubs, or gym memberships.
Relocation & Moving Expenses Treated as taxable income as of January 1, 2018. Use natural account 66040. Imputed income typically reported on the paycheck two months following reimbursement. All lodging for relocation must be submitted on a Travel Expense Report.
Human Subject Study Payments The University prefers payment from petty cash. Employees may be reimbursed for out-of-pocket payments. Compensation of $2,000 or more per participant in calendar year 2026 is taxable and requires Form 1099 reporting with SSN.
Contributions / Donations The University will not reimburse employees for contributions or donations to other organizations unless: it is 501(c)(3) to 501(c)(3), and it has written approval from the President, Provost, a Dean, Vice President, or Senior Financial Officer. No contributions to religious or political organizations.
Payments for Services The University will not reimburse an employee for a payment made to an individual who provided services. These must go through University Payroll or a Purchase Order (for consultants).
Tuition Degree-program tuition not covered by University benefits is generally not acceptable and is taxable. Reasonable professional development/training may be reimbursed if mutually beneficial. Questions on taxation: Jessica Yim (jl6720@columbia.edu).
Sales Tax The University is tax-exempt in certain states. Cardholders should present tax-exempt status at point of purchase. Tax-exempt number is printed on T&E Corporate Card. Contact Finance Service Center at 212-854-2122 for the exemption form or state list.
Group Conference & Venue Bookings Must be reserved by Travel Arrangers using the Corporate Card. Personal credit cards should not be used for these bookings.

Policy Exception Process
If a valid business reason exists for any of these, a policy exception must be requested with SBO approval. Accounts Payable will also accept SBO approval via email attached to the voucher or as an ad-hoc approver in the system.
The Core Standard: All expenses must be for a valid business purpose that is necessary and reasonable to conduct University business. When in doubt, ask: would a knowledgeable third-party reviewer understand why this expense was incurred? If the expense involves external parties, they must have an actual or potential business relationship with the University — and the event must provide a documented benefit.
